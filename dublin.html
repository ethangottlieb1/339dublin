Statues and Architecture Around Dublin

This page is dedicated to my experience in Dublin and how my appreciation grew for all of the beautiful architecure and statues that fill the city with rich history!

Pictures

Press or Click on each image below for some info!

This is an image of The Custom House from afar which shows the beautiful architecture. Press on the enlarged version below to learn more about the history behind that architecture.

This image of the intricate designs on The Custom House showcases the rich history of Ireland. The designs represent boats for transporation, people of importance, and other events that have occurred in Irish history.

This is the Thomas Moore Statue in the Dublin City Centre. Thomas Moore was a famous Irish writer and poet. His fame comes from his Irish Melodies.

This is the intricate design structure on a building at Trinity College. There are all kinds of environmental designs such as flowers, birds, squirrels, lizards, and more.

This is the Campanile in Trinity College. It is a belltower located in the heart of Trinity's campus and it is arguably Trinity's most iconic landmark/statue.

This is the Wellington Monument located directly across the road from the Irish President's house in Phoenix Park. The monument was finished in 1861 and it was built in order to commemorate the 1st Duke of Wellington, Arthur Wellesley.

This is the Spire, a piece of art that was created as a result of an art competition. If you ask anybody that lives here in Dublin, they hate it. It is honestly quite the eye sore.

This is the burial site for famous Irish military men. It is located next to both a church and a maximum security prison!

This is the cross in the grass at Phoenix Park. It represents and commemorates the governmental figures who were brutally murdered at that exact spot in the park. It is known as the most famous murder in Irish history.
